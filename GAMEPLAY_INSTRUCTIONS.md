## Player Guide

### For Players: How to Play

#### BEFORE THE GAME STARTS

**Step 1: Join the Game**
1. Scan the QR code or visit the join link
2. Enter the Game ID provided by host
3. Enter your name (use your real name for easier gameplay)
4. Wait in the lobby until the host starts the game

**Step 2: Wait for Character Assignment**
- The host will assign you a character
- Once assigned, your character info will appear on your screen
- **DO NOT share your screen with anyone!**

**Step 3: Read Your Character Sheet**
You'll see several sections:

ðŸ“– **"About You"** - Your public backstory
- You WILL read this aloud when the host asks
- Everyone will hear this

ðŸ¤ **"Your Secret"** - Private information
- NEVER reveal this unless the game requires it
- This is for your eyes only

ðŸŽ¯ **"Your Actions"** - What to do during the game
- Follow these instructions carefully
- They tell you how to act and what to say

ðŸ•µï¸ **"Your Goal"** - What you're trying to achieve
- This is your mission for the game
- Work toward this goal during investigation

ðŸ’¡ **"Tips"** - Additional hints and secrets
- Read carefully for special information
- May include things you witnessed

#### DURING THE GAME

**Phase 1: Introductions**
- Listen as each player reads their "About You" section
- Take mental notes of everyone's roles
- Pay attention to who had conflicts with the victim

**Phase 2: The Murder**
- Player 1 (Momo King) will perform a death scene
- Lights may be turned off briefly
- The host will announce the murder
- Pay attention to who was where

**Phase 3: Investigation (The Main Game)**

This is the core of the game! Here's how to play:

1. **Stay In Character**
   - Speak and act as your character would
   - Use your character's personality
   - Reference your character's relationships

2. **Follow Your Character's Instructions**
   - Your "Actions" section tells you what to do
   - Some characters need to reveal certain info at certain times
   - Some characters need to defend themselves
   - Some need to accuse others

3. **Investigate Actively**
   - Ask other players questions
   - Share information (but not your secret!)
   - Look for contradictions in alibis
   - Pay attention to who has motive, means, and opportunity

4. **Use Available Resources**
   - Ghost Crystal (ask yes/no questions)
   - Clues revealed by the host
   - Information other players share
   - Your character's special knowledge

5. **What You CAN Share:**
   - Your "About You" information
   - Your alibi
   - Things your "Tips" section says you witnessed
   - Suspicions about others
   - Information from revealed clues

6. **What You CANNOT Share:**
   - Your "Secret" section (unless caught/cornered)
   - Information your character wouldn't know
   - Out-of-character knowledge

7. **Taking Notes**
   - Keep notes on your device or paper
   - Track who was where during the murder
   - Note suspicious behaviors
   - List motives for each player

**Phase 4: Accusations**
- When host calls for accusations, state your case
- Present your evidence
- Vote on who you think is the killer
- The player with most votes is accused

**Phase 5: The Reveal**
- The host will reveal the true killer
- Win conditions:
  - **If you're innocent:** Correctly identify the killer
  - **If you're the killer:** Avoid being caught!

## Investigation Phase Guide

### FOR HOSTS: Managing the Investigation

#### Keep Things Moving

**If discussion stalls:**
- "Let's focus on alibis. Who DOESN'T have a solid alibi?"
- "Has anyone noticed any contradictions?"
- "Ghost, can you give us a hint?" (have P1 point at something)

**If one player is too quiet:**
- "[Player Name], you've been very quiet. What do you think about [recent accusation]?"
- "Player 7, as the Legal Advisor, what's your take on this?"

**If players are stuck:**
- Reveal the next clue early
- Have the Ghost become more animated
- Prompt specific players to reveal information

**If accusations get too heated:**
- "Remember, stay in character everyone!"
- "Let's hear their defense before we jump to conclusions"
- "Back up your accusations with evidence"

#### Clue Timing Guidelines

Release clues based on progress, not strict time:

**Autopsy Report:** When players have discussed motives
**Sleeping Pill Clue:** When players focus too much on scientific poisons
**Text Messages:** When players aren't considering relationship issues
**Witness Statements:** When key evidence isn't emerging

**Player 12 (Killer):**

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue: Players are reading their secrets aloud**
- Solution: "Stop! That section is for your eyes only. Don't reveal your secret unless caught!"

**Issue: Players aren't participating**
- Solution: Direct questions to quiet players: "Player X, what do you think about this accusation?"

**Issue: Discussion is going in circles**
- Solution: Introduce the next clue or refocus: "Let's move on to discussing means. Who had access to poison?"

**Issue: Players are making random accusations without evidence**
- Solution: "In character, what evidence do you have? Your character wouldn't randomly accuse someone."

**Issue: Someone figured it out too quickly**
- Solution: Don't confirm! Let them try to convince others. The fun is in the debate!

**Issue: Players are being too aggressive/personal**
- Solution: "Remember, this is your CHARACTER accusing, not you personally. Keep it fun!"

**Issue: The Ghost (P1) is answering questions incorrectly**
- Solution: Have a cheat sheet showing P12 is the killer. Before game, brief P1 on key answers.

**Issue: Technical problems with devices**
- Solution: Have printed backup character sheets ready

**Issue: Players keep looking at each other's screens**
- Solution: "No peeking! Keep your devices private or the mystery is ruined!"

**Issue: Game is running too long**
- Solution: "We have 15 minutes left, let's start final accusations"

**Issue: Game is going too fast**
- Solution: Add more discussion rounds, slow down clue reveals
